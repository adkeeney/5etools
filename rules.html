    Ground Rules

    While I typically DM using the core 5th edition D&D rules as written, there are a few variations/deviations of note that I've chosen to utilize.

    • Any official 5e adventure can and will be modified at the DM's discretion
    • No excessive infighting, murderhoboing, sexual content. Players in a party are expected to work together. Problem players will be asked to leave. To repeat, all party members must be working together and cooperating.
    • Session Zero is mandatory for each player. You must have a one on one discussion with the DM regarding your character and the game.
    • DM controls the game and has the ultimate decision on all aspects of the game. I'm not a perfect DM, so if I make a ruling you think is wrong you can challenge it with evidence from 5e rules as written/sage advice etc. We may continue play with a ruling decision by the DM and can discuss the issue later to not interrupt play. In the end the DM gets the ultimate decision and that must be respected!
    • This game is Rated 'R.' Cursing is allowed. D&D has themes of darkness, fighting, injury, death and extreme conditions. It also has many different races, genders and social issues that may arise.Â  Bottom line, don't be an asshole. I will not tolerate players utilizing this game to be racist, sexist, transphobic, classist, political, etc etc. The DM can and will remove players at any time for any reason.
    • If you as a player are uncomfortable for any reason during play, please message me privately and we will 'take a short break' and I will address the issue.
    • Ground Rule bottom line: You are playing here at no cost. I as a DM am taking massive amounts of time to craft and run these games. The other players are taking lots of time to play and participate. Be a good team member, show up and participate and have fun!

    Mechanics

    • XP: will be milestone and at the DM's discretion.
    • Leveling up: If you roll a 1 on the die for Hit Points, you can reroll until you get higher than 1.
    • Absence: If one player is absent, the DM or a willing player will run that PC. (Absent players choice.) If more than one player is absent the session will be postponed.
    • Encumbrance: I don't want to micromanage the game like this so use common sense. It is the DM's discretion on carrying large objects/too much weight.
    • Ammunition: Ammunition is tracked and must be purchased.
    • Skill Checks: Multiple skill checks for the same reason causes the DC to rise each time. (i.e - Everybody tries to pick a lock, the DC increases each time)
    • Spell Components: Spell components that have a cost or are consumed must be purchased and accounted for.
    • Firearms: No guns

    Combat

    • Flanking: No flanking
    • Potions: Drinking a potion is a bonus action. Potions given or administered to others is an action.
    • Initiative: Enemies each roll their own initiative. No group rolls.

    This is not an all inclusive list of rule variations. I try to run the majority of things RAW but there will be certain things that are tweaked. THE DM HAS FULL DISCRETION ON RULES

    A note on evil characters

    I just want to be clear what my definition of an 'Evil' character is and set the expectation that you must strictly adhere to my definition if you want to be 'Evil'

    An 'Evil' character must still cooperate with the party towards goals. In-party fighting and non-cooperation leads to game-play that becomes not fun. A person can be 'Evil' and still be loyal to the party. It's your job in your background to find a way to incorporate why you would go along with this party despite being selfish and 'Evil'. You must cooperate with the party.

    Being 'Evil' does not equate to killing everyone you see just for fun, murdering commoners and just straight playing like a monster. 'Evil' is more of a character that only does thing for selfish reasons. You may be trying to gain wealth, power or fame. You may be manipulative and lie for this personal gain. Being 'Evil' does not mean you oppose anything good, it just means you may not care about events or people that don't align with your personal goals.

    Being 'Evil' does not give you the right to just play as an uncooperative, disruptive asshole.

    Characters who choose to be 'Evil' will be under more scrutiny than others. I just don't want this to devolve into a PC who starts killing babies, torturing people, killing NPC's unnecessarily and just generally being an asshole.
